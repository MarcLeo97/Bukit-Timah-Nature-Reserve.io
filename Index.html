Bukit Timah Nature Reserve

This 163-ha reserve includes Singapore’s highest hill, Bukit Timah Hill, which stands at 163 m and retains one of the few areas of primary rainforest in the country. The forest on the hill has been a botanical collection ground for more than a century, and the first known specimens of many species of Malayan plants have been obtained here. It was officially declared an ASEAN Heritage Park on 18 October 2011.



Things to do

Things to do

1. Trekking up to Bukit Timah Summit

Enjoy the beautiful greeneries such as our nation native lora and fauna species include the Shorea curtisii, Red Dhup (Parishia insignis), Greater Racket-tailed Drongo (Dicrurus paradiseus) and Malayan Colugo (Galeopterus variegatus) while trekking your way up to the summit.

2. Be advanterous and take on the Mountain Bike Trail

Steep lung-busting climbs and sharp descents through lush tropical terrain marked by muddy corners and gnarly roots in the heart of the Central Catcment Area are just some of the experiences one can expect on this 6.5-kilometre trail circumnavigating the 164-metre tall Bukit Timah Hill.

Click here for the Bike Trail map



3. Take Instagrammable Photos at Hindhede Quarry

"A short, paved boardwalk trail that is accessible from the visitor centre loops around the compact park and takes you through the rainforest, offering spectacular breath taking views of Hindhede Quarry from “Look Out Point”. It is easily accessible for families with young children or elderly.

Click here for the map



4. Playground in the Nature

Bring your young ones to enjoy to the playground within Hindhede Nature Park which is located at the bottom of the hill, beside the visitor centre, offers plenty of climbing structures and a super long zipline/flying fox. There are also 3-4 picnic tables and a grassy clearing that’s perfect for getting the wiggles out.
